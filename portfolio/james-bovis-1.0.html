My first version of jamesbovis.com

Back in 2015 it was my new year's resolution to finally purchase and launch my own personal website on jamesbovis.com. The idea being that I needed a place to share my work and for potential employers to get a copy of my CV and see other things about me.

Along with the website successfully launching that year, I also needed a logo which would feature across the website, on business cards and on other materials.

My logo must haves

To start with, I ironed out some “must have” credentials which I would stick to when designing my logo and these three things gave me a starting point to work on.

  • Combine my love of Web and Graphic design

  • Be memorable and easy to identify

  • Be flexible

After these three principles were set out, I got to work

Some Ideas

I started on paper. Firstly, writing out my name in full, rearranging the two words in different ways to see what looked the best. Luckily for me, both my forename and surname have the same about of letters in them, so I decided that I should go for a “stacked” approach.

One thing that I wanted to include into the logo from the outset was using HTML closing tags somewhere in the design.

The final idea


After a few more sketches and prototypes in illustrator, I was finally satisfied with the final outcome.

Conclusion

Once I reached the end of the design process, I am confident in saying that my logo achieved the three goals I outlined earlier. I think that the use of the pencil and the styled elements above the “M” really convey my design and development skills.

I also chose a simple color palette which would ensure easy use on both web and in print and I think the colours look great together.
